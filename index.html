Jane Austen

In case you're interested in Jane Austen then you should watch this video about her life.

The main character is Elizabeth Bennet; a strong minded and quite honestly a prejudiced individule. She is particularly prejudiced against a man named Mr. Darcy who she thinks is the most awful and prideful person she could ever meet. Elizabeth's family never ceases to embarace her excepting her older sister, Jane. Elizabeth goes through a change of heart and watches a few of her sisters go through romances. Ultimately her and Darcy fall in love and get married.

Pride and Prejudice is perhaps my favorite book. It's a love story as all of Austen's works are, but so far is, I think, the best of hers. This is a well written and creative story that I consider a classic. At first the big words and long paragraphs seem intimidating but if you persevere it is a very enjoyable tale and I recomend it to readers looking for a challenge and a book outside of what you might find common today.

Sense and Sensibility is about Elinor Dashwood and her family; When her father dies Elinor's Mother and two younger sisters, as well as herself, no longer have very many means of supporting themselves so they have to move to a small cottage. Elinor is a resigned and sensible individual whereas her sister, Marianne, believes completely in always embracing er emotions to the fullest. As you may imagine Marianne goes through heart break of the worst kind, but even cautious Elinor finds herself going through perilous and confusing emotions that she is sworn not to express, not even to her close sister Marianne. They are fortunate however to end up happily married to worthy men.

Though I do not think it's as good as Pride and Prejudice it is definetly a close second; The only inferiority I find is in the character development of Elinor Dashwood. I recommend this book to people who enjoy romance novels with big words and complicated feelings.

Mansfield Park is a wonderful story about how a girl named Fanny, born into a poor family with several siblings goes to live with one of her wealthier aunts, Lady Bertram. She grows up with her 4 older cousins, two boys and two girls. Thanks to her inferior birth she has never been treated as an equal by any of her extended family. Her other aunt Mrs. Grant, who lives nearby treats her horribly and always finds fault with her.Through it all Fanny is quiet, reserved, and kind despite her ill treatment. She watches as her extended family make mistake after mistake and she suffers through the proposals and pining of Mrs. Grants younger half brother, Henry Crawford. She eventually visits her real family and finds them to be more unruly and indifferent to her than she thought they were after being away for so many years. Despite all of this she ends up marrying the only man she'd ever loved, Edmund.

I loved, loved, loved, loved this book! However, I admit, I hated the ending. I really wanted Fanny to marry Henry Crawford, not Edmund! I just thought it was so sweet how he tried so hard to earn Fanny's love, but she just ended up rejecting him over and over. Anyways I thought this was really relatable because of how shy she was and how often she was forgotten because I think a lot of shy people go through the same thing.

Unfortunatly I have not finished reading this book.

Frank Peretti

There are strange things going on in this Washington High School; Students are becoming sick, like they're being possesed. So, an expert team of undercover detectives are called in to find not just the solution, but the truth; They're a christian family, two parents and twin teenagers, Elijah and Elishah. The kids go into the school as students and find some interesting things about the kids and the curriculum. They eventualy find what's making the kids go crazy but not before a student dies unexpectedly. Then they had to find who was unleashing the mennacc, but not without a close call to Elishah's life and throwing a friend in jail.

A terrific book and easy read I would recommend it to anyone who likes a good mystery and sound christian morals. My only problem with this book is that the highschool setting is not portrayed properly; at least not compared to my experiences. In the book the students are grouped in the classic way; dumb jocks, snobby cheerleaders, weak nerds, bullied geeks, and creepy witchs. First off I have yet to see any witchs in any educational setting, but second is that I hate the stereotypes because smart kids can do sports, sporty people can be smart and nice, and nerds and geeks are not always as socially inept as often portrayed. Still a good book though.

If you thought 'Hangman's Curse' was strange then 'Nightmare Acadamey' will knock your socks off. When a young man and his friend run away from home there are obvious concerns and questions that arise. However they're nothing compared to the ones that come up when they find him alone and totally insane; and then mysteriously killed shortly after seeing his parents and uttering two strange words: "Nightmare Acadamey." The same family of four are called in to investigate and find an interesting government project for kids who volunteer for a strange school program that has twisted into the footworking of world domination. Kids are ubducted and then allowed to be whoever they want(some literally change their names everyday) and are given no rules, no requirements, no morals, just what they think is right. Obviously havoc breaks out and in the end the 'teachers' almost blow up everybody, including Elijah and Elisha, but Elijah saves them all despite his scrambled brain caused by a cruel experiment by the 'teachers'(more like torturers) to show some interested parties that though christians are the hardest to crack, they too can eventually befall to complete controle by an outside party; But what do they know, he's the one saved the day after all. Naturally the good guys win and bad guys lose, happily ever after.

Terrific book! So creative, could not put it down, it was always "I'll just read until I know the answer to this question" but there was always another question! I recommend to anyone that likes an easy read and loves a good mystery with a happy ending but troubling middle; and of course you can't forget the safe and sound christian morals.

Chris Colfer

At the twins', Conner and Alex Bailey, 12th birthday their Grandma comes to visit and gives them a treasured fairytale book. Alex soon discovers that the book is humming and glowing and when she investigates by placing pencils and books on it, they disapear. Then when Alex accidently falls through the book Conner jumps in after her. The book acts as a portal into another world of all the stories they grew up reading; Cinderella, Snow White, Rapunzel, Aurora, Goldilocks, and little red riding hood. Their immediate concern is to get back home and they find that the only way is to use a spell so they have to go on a treasure hunt to find all of the items. But they're not the only ones on the hunt, The Evil Queen from the story of Snow White, wants to use the 'Wishing Spell' to free her lover from his prison in a mirror. Unfortunately the Evil Queen is successful only to have Mira die in her arms. The twins however are pleasantly surprised when they find out that low and behold their Grandmother is the famous Fairy Godmother of Cinderella and they are soon escorted home through her magic and reunited with their mom.

Pam Munoz Ryan

Esperanza lives like a princess in Mexico on her family's ranch, but one night her loving father doesn't come home. Not long after her father's death Esperanza's evil uncles try to take over the ranch when one of them asks to marry her mother. Of course her mother says no but that night their large home is burned to the ground, nothing left for Esperanza to remember him by but a doll. Esperanza, her mother, and a family of servants that they are very close with, leave for California to work. Esperanza didn't even know how to sweep with a broom when she got there but when her mother gets sick she goes out to work in the fields, watches babies, cleans, and shops. She saves and saves her money to help her mother and pay doctor bills. It's hard but even harder is when a very close friend of hers takes all of her money and leaves. Of course everyone was angry but then he returns with Esperanza's beloved grandmother who couldn't make the trip before. Then Esperanza's mother, who had been struggling to fight her illness without her mother, finally starts to improve and after such a very long and trying time Esperanza finally feels happy again.

Classic rags to riches story but so well displayed in a real and unique way. This is set during the Great Depression and I don't mention it in the deascription but there is a lot of conflict and pressure from the people she meets to go on strike for better pay and all of the options that presents; and of course there is a lot of inequality and racism towardsd her and the people in her camp.

This is a table of what I think are the most common genres entertainment wise.
Genre Definition
Sci-Fi fiction based on imagined future scientific or technological advances and major social or environmental changes, frequently portraying space or time travel and life on other planets.
Drama a composition in prose or verse presenting in dialogue or pantomime a story involving conflict or contrast of character, especially one intended to be acted on the stage; a play. the branch of literature having such compositions as its subject; dramatic art or representation.
Action and Andventure An adventure is an event or series of events that happens outside the course of the protagonist's ordinary life, usually accompanied by danger, often by physical action.
Romance In traditional literary terms, a narration of the extraordinary exploits of heroes, often in exotic or mysterious settings.
Mystery Mystery is a genre of fiction in which a detective, either an amateur or a professional, solves a crime or a series of crimes.
Horror Horror is a genre of fiction which is intended to, or has the capacity to frighten, scare, disgust, or startle their readers or viewers by inducing feelings of horror and terror.
Historical Fiction in which a story is made up but is set in the past and sometimes borrows true characteristics of the time period in which it is set.
Fantasy Fantasy is a fiction genre set in an imaginary universe, often but not always without any locations, events, or people from the real world.
